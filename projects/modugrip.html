ModuGrip

Modular robotic gripping system designed for adaptability and scalability.

Overview

ModuGrip is a modular gripping system designed to adapt to different object shapes and use cases. The project focuses on flexibility, reusability, and mechanical problem solving rather than a single-purpose design.

Objectives Demonstrated

Objective 1: Design and complete robotic and embedded systems solutions that apply to real-world situations and challenges.

ModuGrip meets this objective by addressing a real robotics need for adaptable gripping across varying use cases. Instead of building a one-purpose design, the project focuses on a more flexible solution that could support broader robotic applications in real environments.

Objective 3: Demonstrate embedded system design skills, including, but not limited to, microcontroller selection, schematic design, printed circuit board layout, design for electromagnetic compatibility and design for manufacturing.

While more mechanically focused than some of the other projects, ModuGrip still supports this objective through design-for-build thinking and engineering decision-making intended for integration into larger robotic systems. The project reflects practical planning, modularity, and system-minded design choices relevant to robotics development.
